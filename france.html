Introduction

As one of the most modern countries in the world, France plays an influencial role as a leader among European nations. To resistant instabilities, France constructed a hybrid presidential-parliamentary governing system since 1958.

The introduction of a common currency, the euro, has proved that France was the central to the economic integration of Europe.

The reconciliation and cooperation with Germany also proved that France played an important role as a permanent member of the United Nationa Security Council, NATO, the G-7, the G-20, the EU, and other multilateral organizations.

President:Emmanuel Macron

Capital:Paris

Geography

Location

Metropolitan France: the majority of France's territory and population in Western Europe; it is bordered by the Bay of Biscay and English Channel, betweeen Belgium and Spain, southeast of the UK.

Five overseas regions:

  • French Guiana in Northern South America
  • Guadeloupe in Caribbean: Southeast of Puerto Rico
  • Martinique in Caribbean: North of Trinidad and Tobago
  • Mayotte in Southern Indian Ocean
  • Reunion in Southern Africa

Coastline

4,853 km

metropolitan France: 3,427 km

Climate

Metropolitan France: cool winters and mild summers, but also mild winters and hot summers along the Mediterranean

French Guiana: tropical; little seasonal temperature

Guadeloupe and Martinique: rainy season from June to October, hurricanes every eight years on average

Mayotte: tropical; rainy season during northeastern monsoon from November to May; cooler bbut dry season from May to November

Reunion: cool and dry from May to Nnovember; hot and rainy from November to April
